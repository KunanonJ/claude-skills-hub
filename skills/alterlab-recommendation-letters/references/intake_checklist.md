# Dossier Intake Checklist

A recommendation letter is only as good as the dossier behind it. Before drafting,
collect (or extract from what the user already provided) the fields below. **Any
field you cannot fill from the supplied material becomes a bracketed gap in the
draft — never invented content.**

---

## Required fields

1. **Letter type** — one of: graduate admission, faculty hiring,
   tenure/promotion external review, fellowship, award nomination. This sets the
   reader, register, and structure (see `letter_types.md`).
2. **Candidate** — full name and current role/title.
3. **Target** — the program, position, fellowship, or award, and the institution
   or body, plus any **specific prompts** the requester asked the letter to
   address (committees often list these explicitly).
4. **Relationship** — how the recommender knows the candidate, **in what
   capacity**, and **for how long**. This anchors the opening and the writer's
   standing to assess. For external review, also: any conflict of interest and
   whether the relationship is arms-length.

## Evidence the letter will be built from

5. **Accomplishments** — the candidate's relevant achievements, with any **metrics,
   artifacts, dates, or anecdotes** the recommender can attest to. Push for
   *specific instances*, not adjectives: a moment the candidate showed
   independence, a problem they solved, a setback they handled.
6. **Qualities to address** — the dimensions the target asks about (e.g. research
   independence, teaching, collegiality, leadership) and which the recommender can
   speak to from direct knowledge.
7. **Comparisons the recommender can honestly make** — any ranking against a real
   population the writer knows (their own advisees, reviewees, a cohort). Capture
   the **population and basis**, not just the percentile.

## Logistics

8. **Length / format norms** — page limit, format, submission portal or address,
   and **deadline**. Honor an explicit instruction from the requester over the
   default norms in `letter_types.md`.
9. **Tone constraints** — any institution-specific requirements (e.g. arms-length
   language, required statement on conflicts, signature/letterhead expectations).

---

## Turning gaps into bracketed asks

For every required or evidence field that is missing or thin, do **two** things:

- Insert an inline bracketed placeholder where the content would go, naming
  exactly what is needed, e.g.
  `[recommender: one concrete example of the candidate's research independence]`.
- Add the item to a short **"facts still needed"** list returned alongside the
  draft.

Do not smooth over a gap with a plausible invention. The recommender signs the
letter; the facts must be theirs.

---

## Quick intake prompt (paste-ready)

> To draft this letter I need: (1) letter type; (2) candidate name + role;
> (3) the program/position/award + institution, and any prompts the requester
> listed; (4) how you know the candidate, in what capacity, and for how long;
> (5) 2–4 specific accomplishments or moments you can attest to (with metrics or
> anecdotes if you have them); (6) the qualities the requester wants addressed;
> (7) any honest ranking you can make and the population it's drawn from;
> (8) length/format and the deadline. Anything you can't give me I'll leave as a
> bracketed gap for you to fill — I won't invent it.
