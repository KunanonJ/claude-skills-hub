# No-Cost Extensions & Rebudgeting / Virement

Two related post-award requests. Both reallocate *time* or *money* within an award
that is already made — neither asks for new funds or new scope. Get that framing
right and the request is usually routine; get it wrong (implying new money or new
aims) and it is rejected.

---

## No-Cost Extension (NCE)

An NCE extends the project end date **without** additional funds, to finish the
already-approved scope.

### What a funder needs to grant an NCE

1. **Programmatic justification** — *why* more time is needed (a delay in hiring,
   data collection, equipment delivery, IRB/ethics approval, fieldwork season,
   etc.). "We have funds left" is **not** a sufficient reason on its own.
2. **Unobligated balance** — how much money remains and how it will be spent in the
   extension period.
3. **Revised timeline** — the remaining milestones/deliverables and the new end
   date.
4. **Explicit confirmation** — no new scope, no new objectives, no new funds
   requested.

### Justification template

```
Subject: No-Cost Extension request — {award number}, {PI}

Current end date: {date}    Requested new end date: {date} ({N} months)

1. Reason for the extension
   {The programmatic cause of the delay and why it could not be foreseen/avoided.}

2. Remaining work
   {The specific aims/milestones/deliverables still to be completed.}

3. Unobligated balance and spending plan
   Estimated remaining funds: {amount}. These will be used for {personnel /
   data collection / publication / ...} during the extension period.

4. No change to scope or budget
   No new objectives, no change in scope, and no additional funds are requested.
```

### Funder notes

- **NIH** — a grantee under expanded authorities may exercise a **first** no-cost
  extension of up to **12 months** by notifying NIH (no prior approval); a
  **second** or longer extension requires NIH prior approval. Confirm against the
  Notice of Award and the NIH Grants Policy Statement.
- **NSF** — the awardee may authorize a **one-time extension of up to 12 months**
  (grantee-approved NCE) via Research.gov; any **further** extension is an
  NSF-approved NCE requiring NSF review. Confirm against the PAPPG and award
  conditions.
- **Horizon Europe / ERC** — the project duration is amended through a **Grant
  Agreement amendment** on the Funding & Tenders Portal; justify it and confirm
  against the Grant Agreement and the Online Manual.

> Extension *limits and whether prior approval is needed* vary by award. Present
> the generic rule, then tell the user to confirm against their own award terms;
> do not assert a specific allowance the user's documents do not support.

## Rebudgeting / Virement

Moving funds **between budget categories** (or, in EU projects, between
beneficiaries) within the same total award.

### What to state

1. **From → to** — the source category and the destination category, with amounts.
2. **Reason** — why the original allocation no longer fits the work (ties back to
   the variance narrative — see `budget_variance.md`).
3. **Scope unchanged** — confirm the aims/objectives are unaffected.
4. **Prior-approval check** — whether the move is within the awardee's own
   authority or needs the funder's prior approval (this is the part that varies
   most by funder/award — confirm, don't assume).

### Common prior-approval triggers (verify per award)

- Crossing into or out of categories the funder controls (e.g. equipment,
  participant support, subawards).
- Changing the level of effort of key personnel beyond a stated threshold.
- Changes that effectively alter scope.

### Template

```
Rebudgeting request — {award number}

Move {amount} from {source category} to {destination category}.
Reason: {programmatic, cross-referencing the variance narrative}.
Scope impact: none — aims and deliverables unchanged.
Approval path: {within awardee authority | funder prior approval required} —
to be confirmed against the Notice of Award / Grant Agreement.
```

---

## Self-check (both requests)

- Is it crystal-clear that **no new funds and no new scope** are requested?
- Is the reason **programmatic**, not merely "money/time left over"?
- Are the amounts and dates the user's real figures or `[TODO]` placeholders?
- Does the request name the **approval path** and tell the user to confirm it
  against their award documents rather than asserting a fixed threshold?
