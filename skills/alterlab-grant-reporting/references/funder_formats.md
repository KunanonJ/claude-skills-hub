# Funder Report Formats

Per-funder structure for post-award technical/progress reports. Use the funder's
own section letters and titles verbatim — funders reject reports that merge,
rename, or reorder sections. Every fact below is sourced inline.

---

## NIH — Research Performance Progress Report (RPPR)

Submitted in **eRA Commons**. One section skeleton (A–I) serves three report
types. Section titles are from the NIH RPPR Instruction Guide (eRA, January 2026).

| § | Section title | What it covers |
|---|---------------|----------------|
| A | Cover Page | PD/PI, organization, project title, reporting period |
| B | Accomplishments | Major goals; what was accomplished; opportunities for training; results dissemination; plans for the next period |
| C | Products | Publications, conference papers, websites, technologies/techniques, inventions/patents, datasets, software |
| D | Participants | Personnel and their effort (person-months); other support changes |
| E | Impact | Impact on the principal discipline, on other disciplines, on technology transfer, and on society |
| F | Changes | Changes in approach and reasons; problems/delays; significant changes to protections/approvals; **scope and rebudgeting changes are surfaced here** |
| G | Special Reporting Requirements | Responsible-conduct training, human-subjects/inclusion, foreign component, etc. |
| H | Budget | Budget for the next period — **Annual (non-SNAP) only** |
| I | Outcomes | Concise, public-facing summary of project outcomes |

### Section applicability by report type

- **Annual RPPR** — describes scientific progress, significant changes, personnel,
  and plans for the next budget period/year. Section H (Budget) appears on
  **non-SNAP** annual reports.
- **Interim RPPR** — filed when a competing renewal is submitted. If the renewal is
  funded, it serves as the annual report for the final year of the prior
  competitive segment; if not funded, it becomes the Final report.
- **Final RPPR** — part of closeout. Adds **Section I (Outcomes)**. On Interim and
  Final RPPRs, **D.1 is the only Section D item required**, and Section H (Budget)
  does **not** apply.

Sources:
- NIH, "Research Performance Progress Report (RPPR)", grants.nih.gov —
  post-award monitoring & reporting.
- NIH RPPR Instruction Guide (eRA, January 2026) — section titles A–I.
- eRA, "Research Performance Progress Reports (RPPR)",
  https://www.era.nih.gov/recipients/submit-reports/rppr.htm — the three report
  types and their purpose.

### NIH RPPR deadlines

- **SNAP** (Streamlined Non-competing Award Process) annual RPPR — due
  **≈ 45 days before** the next budget period start date.
- **Non-SNAP** annual RPPR — due **≈ 60 days before** the next budget period start
  date.
- **Multi-Year Funded (MYF)** RPPR — due annually on/before the anniversary of the
  budget/project period start date.
- **Final RPPR** — due **within 120 days** of the period-of-performance end date.

Source: NIH/eRA RPPR guidance (deadlines as summarized by NIH and institutional
sponsored-programs offices, e.g. UNC OSP, Harvard OSP).

## NSF — Project Reports

Submitted in **Research.gov**. Three report products:

- **Annual project report** — covers that year's activities related to the funded
  project plus broader-impacts work. Due **no later than 90 days before** the end
  of the current reporting budget period.
- **Final project report** — refers **only to the final funded year** of the
  project; it is **not** a cumulative report. Due **no later than 120 days
  following** the award end date.
- **Project Outcomes Report (POR)** — a summary of **no more than 800 words** on
  the nature and outcomes of the project, written for a **public audience**. Due
  **no later than 120 days following** the award end date. Separate from the final
  project report.

Sources:
- NSF, "Report Your Progress and Outcomes",
  https://www.nsf.gov/awards/report-your-outcomes.
- Institutional sponsored-programs guidance (e.g. MIT RAS, WUSTL Financial
  Services) restating the 90-day / 120-day / 800-word rules.

## Horizon Europe / ERC — Periodic & Final Reports

Submitted on the **EU Funding & Tenders Portal** (Continuous & Periodic Reporting
modules). The report has two parts:

### Technical Report

- **Part A** — structured tables with project information; **generated by the IT
  system** from what the beneficiary enters into the Portal's Continuous &
  Periodic Reporting modules.
- **Part B** — the **narrative**; it mirrors the application form and requires the
  participants to **report on differences / deviations** from the planned work.

### Financial Report

- **Individual and consolidated Financial Statements** for each beneficiary.
- A **Certificate on the Financial Statements (CFS)** where the threshold applies.
- Report on the use of resources.

### Continuous Reporting module

Open throughout the project; information entered there transfers automatically to
the Periodic Reporting module (and feeds Part A).

### Deadlines

- **Periodic report** (and the **final** periodic report) — due **within 60 days
  after** the end of each reporting period. A typical final period covers project
  months 31–60.

Sources:
- European Research Council, "Project Reporting",
  https://erc.europa.eu/manage-your-project/project-reporting — 60-day deadline,
  scientific + financial parts, continuous reporting.
- EU Funding & Tenders "Reports and payment requests" online manual and the
  official Horizon/Euratom Periodic Report template — Part A / Part B structure
  and Financial Statements.

## Cross-funder drafting rules

- **Tie every accomplishment to a stated aim / objective / deliverable.** Reviewers
  read the report against the funded workplan.
- **Report deviations explicitly.** EU Part B is *built* around differences;
  NIH Section F and NSF reports both expect changes/problems to be named, not
  hidden.
- **Keep report scope correct.** NSF final report = final funded year only; NIH
  Final RPPR adds Outcomes; EU final periodic report covers its final period.
- **Never fabricate numbers or products.** Leave `[TODO: confirm]` placeholders for
  any figure, date, person-month, publication, or DOI the user has not supplied.
