# Closing the Loop — Continuous-Improvement Narratives

"Closing the loop" is the difference between *collecting* assessment data and
*using* it. Both accreditors look for the **action**: AACSB Standard 5.1 expects
"evidence of actions taken to remediate" where competencies are not achieved;
ABET Criterion 4 requires that assessment/evaluation results "are used to effect
continuous improvement." A report full of charts that changes nothing is **not** a
closed loop.

## The loop structure

Write each outcome's improvement story in four moves:

1. **Result** — the measured value against the stated target. State the measure,
   the cohort/n, the result, and whether the target was met. (Numbers come from
   the user; mark missing ones `[TODO: confirm]`.)
2. **Interpretation** — *why* the result looks the way it does. Triangulate the
   direct measure with the indirect measure (e.g. a low direct score that an
   employer survey also flags is a stronger signal than either alone).
3. **Action** — the specific change made or planned: curriculum change (revise a
   course, add an assignment, resequence the I→R→M map), process change (revise
   the rubric, move the assessment point), or resource change. Name who is
   responsible and the date.
4. **Re-assessment** — when the outcome will be measured again to confirm the
   action worked. This is what *closes* the loop; without it the loop is open.

A closed loop is a cycle: the re-assessment becomes the next period's **Result**.

> **Outcome PLO3 — communicate effectively with a range of audiences.**
> **Result:** In the AY2025 capstone (BUS480, n = 38), 62% of students scored
> "proficient" or above on the oral-communication trait of the outcome rubric,
> against a target of 75% — **target not met**. The alumni survey indirect
> measure echoed this: communication was the lowest-rated competency among recent
> graduates.
> **Interpretation:** Students met the written-communication traits but
> under-performed on adapting a message to a non-expert audience; the curriculum
> map showed the outcome was Reinforced in two courses but only first practiced
> for a mixed audience at the capstone.
> **Action:** Beginning AY2026, BUS320 adds a structured presentation to a
> non-specialist panel (moving PLO3 from `R` to a second `R`/`M` point earlier in
> the sequence); the rubric trait descriptors were sharpened. Owner: curriculum
> committee; effective Fall 2026.
> **Re-assessment:** PLO3 re-measured in the AY2027 capstone against the same 75%
> target to confirm the earlier intervention raised attainment.

## Questions each accreditor asks

**AACSB (Standard 5.1):**

- Are competencies **largely achieved**? Where not, is there **evidence of actions
  taken to remediate**?
- Do the AoL results **lead to curricular and process improvements** (5.1)?
- Is the process **systematic and mission-informed**, rather than a once-off
  reporting exercise? (AACSB explicitly does not reward assessing everything every
  year — it rewards meaningful improvement.)
- Are **both direct and indirect** measures represented across the portfolio, with
  a stated rationale for the mix?

**ABET (Criterion 4):**

- Is there a **documented, regularly applied** process for assessing **and
  evaluating** the extent to which the student outcomes are attained?
- Are the **results used to effect continuous improvement** of the program?
- Is the loop tied back to **Criterion 3** student outcomes (and, upward, to the
  Criterion 2 program educational objectives)?

## Common failure modes (flag these when drafting)

- **Data without action.** Results are reported but no change follows — the single
  most common finding. Every not-met target needs an action.
- **Action without re-assessment.** A change is made but never re-measured, so the
  loop never closes.
- **Course-grades-as-assessment.** Reporting GPA or pass rates instead of
  outcome-level direct measures. Program assessment is separate from grading.
- **Indirect-only evidence (AACSB).** Resting the portfolio on surveys with no
  direct measure of performance.
- **Paraphrased outcomes (ABET).** Re-wording a numbered student outcome instead
  of quoting the commission's exact text — see
  [`accreditor_standards.md`](accreditor_standards.md).
- **Inventing figures.** Never fill a target, score, or cohort size to make a
  narrative read well. Mark unknowns `[TODO: confirm]` and let the faculty supply
  the real numbers.
