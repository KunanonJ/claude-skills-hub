## AACSB — 2020 Standard 5: Assurance of Learning

In the **2020 Guiding Principles and Standards for Business Accreditation**, the
nine standards are grouped; the curriculum/learning cluster is:

| Standard | Title |
|----------|-------|
| 4 | Curriculum |
| **5** | **Assurance of Learning** |
| 6 | Learner Progression |
| 7 | Teaching Effectiveness and Impact |

### Standard 5 sub-standards (verbatim headings)

- **5.1** — The school uses well-documented assurance of learning (AoL) processes
  that include direct and indirect measures for ensuring the quality of all
  degree programs that are deemed in scope for accreditation purposes. The
  results of the school's AoL work leads to curricular and process improvements.
- **5.2** — Programs resulting in the same degree credential are structured and
  designed to ensure equivalence of high-quality outcomes irrespective of
  location and modality of instructional delivery. *(Degree Equivalency)*
- **5.3** — Microlearning credentials that are "stackable" or otherwise able to be
  combined into an AACSB-accredited degree program should include processes to
  ensure high quality and continuous improvement. *(Stackable Microlearning
  Credentials)*
- **5.4** — Non-degree executive education that generates greater than five
  percent of a school's total annual resources should include processes to ensure
  high quality and continuous improvement. *(Non-Degree Executive Education)*

### Key definitions (verbatim from the Standard 5 "Definitions" section)

- **Assurance of learning (AoL)** — "the systematic processes and assessment plans
  that collectively demonstrate that learners achieve learning competencies for
  the programs in which they participate that are within the scope of the school's
  accreditation. AoL also includes the processes of identifying competency gaps
  and designing and implementing changes to the curriculum and learning experience
  so the learning competencies are met. AACSB accreditation is concerned with
  broad, program-level, focused competency goals for each degree program, rather
  than detailed competency goals by course or topic."
- **Direct measures** — "evidence from learner work such as examinations, quizzes,
  assignments, and internship or externship feedback that is based on direct
  observation of individual performance behaviors or outcomes."
- **Indirect measures** — "evidence attained from third-party input that is not
  based on direct observation of individual performance behaviors or outcomes,"
  e.g. an employer survey assessing how a school's learners performed on
  internships relative to peers. (Other examples in the Basis for Judgment:
  external outcome measures, focus groups, and interviews.)

### Standard 5.1 — Basis for Judgment (the requirements that drive the package)

- The school **identifies learning competencies for each business degree program**
  plus appropriate **direct and indirect measures** that are **systematically
  assessed** to demonstrate competencies are achieved across degree programs.
- Competencies **derive from and are consonant with the school's mission**,
  strategies, and expected outcomes, and are **reported at the degree level, not
  the major level**.
- Competencies and curriculum-management processes reflect currency of knowledge
  and **stakeholder expectations** (employers, alumni, learners, the university
  community, policymakers).
- Competencies are **largely achieved**; where not achieved, the school provides
  **evidence of actions taken to remediate** the deficiencies. *(This is the
  closing-the-loop requirement.)*
- **Both direct and indirect measures are employed** across the entire portfolio
  of assessment of all degree programs. The proportion of direct vs indirect per
  program is the school's choice, consistent with its mission; the school provides
  its rationale.

### AACSB's "principles-based" stance (do not over-engineer)

AACSB's position: "more complexity, a greater number of competency goals, or
assessing every competency every year does **not** make an AoL process better."
What defines excellence is a **systematic, mission-informed process that leads to
meaningful improvements in learning**, with learners substantially meeting the
stated competencies. Peer-review teams judge against the **spirit and intent** of
Standard 5 and whether the school shows continuous improvement.

## ABET — Criteria 2, 3, 4

ABET's general criteria link three criteria for the AoL story:

| Criterion | Title | Role in AoL |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| 2 | Program Educational Objectives | What graduates are expected to attain a few years after graduation |
| **3** | **Student Outcomes** | What students are able to do by the time of graduation |
| **4** | **Continuous Improvement** | The assessment + evaluation loop that uses outcome data to improve the program |

### Criterion 3 — Student Outcomes (Engineering / EAC), verbatim (1)–(7)

These are the seven required student outcomes in the ABET **Criteria for
Accrediting Engineering Programs** (EAC). Quote them exactly:

1. "an ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems by
   applying principles of engineering, science, and mathematics."
2. "an ability to apply engineering design to produce solutions that meet
   specified needs with consideration of public health, safety, and welfare, as
   well as global, cultural, social, environmental, and economic factors."
3. "an ability to communicate effectively with a range of audiences."
4. "an ability to recognize ethical and professional responsibilities in
   engineering situations and make informed judgments, which must consider the
   impact of engineering solutions in global, economic, environmental, and
   societal contexts."
5. "an ability to function effectively on a team whose members together provide
   leadership, create a collaborative environment, establish goals, plan tasks,
   and meet objectives."
6. "an ability to develop and conduct appropriate experimentation, analyze and
   interpret data, and use engineering judgment to draw conclusions."
7. "an ability to acquire and apply new knowledge as needed, using appropriate
   learning strategies."

> **Commission caveat.** Other ABET commissions — Computing (CAC), Applied &
> Natural Science (ANSAC), Engineering Technology (ETAC) — publish a
> **differently-worded** student-outcome set. Use the exact outcome list for the
> program's actual commission and accreditation cycle. If you cannot confirm the
> exact wording for that commission/cycle, say so and ask — do not invent or
> port the EAC seven into a non-engineering self-study.

### Criterion 4 — Continuous Improvement

ABET's Criterion 4 requires a documented process of **regularly assessing and
evaluating** the extent to which the student outcomes are being attained, with
the **results used to effect continuous improvement** of the program. In AoL
terms: collect outcome data → evaluate against targets → act on the findings →
re-assess. This is the ABET-side "closing the loop"; see
[`closing_the_loop.md`](closing_the_loop.md).

## What goes in the self-study, by accreditor

| AoL artifact | AACSB (Standard 5) | ABET (Criterion 3/4) |
|--------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Outcome set | Program **competency goals**, degree-level, mission-derived | **Student outcomes** (commission's published list) |
| Curriculum map | Competencies → courses (school's scheme) | Student outcomes → courses (I/R/M or assessed map) |
| Direct measures | Required across portfolio (exams, assignments, internship eval) | Required: embedded/direct measures of outcome attainment |
| Indirect measures | Required across portfolio (surveys, focus groups, interviews) | Used as supporting evidence |
| Loop | "Actions taken to remediate" where competencies not achieved | Criterion 4 evaluation → improvement actions |
